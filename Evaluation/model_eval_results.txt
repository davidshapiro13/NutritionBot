In Sudan, a cheap and healthy meal you can prepare for dinner is **Lentil Stew with Vegetables** served with **Injera** or **Flatbread**. This meal is nutritious, filling, and utilizes locally available ingredients.

### Ingredients:
- **Lentils** (red or green)
- **Onion** (1 medium)
- **Garlic** (2-3 cloves)
- **Tomatoes** (fresh or canned)
- **Carrots** (1-2, diced)
- **Spinach or Kale** (a handful, chopped)
- **Vegetable oil** (for sautéing)
- **Spices** (cumin, coriander, turmeric, salt, and pepper)
- **Water or vegetable broth**

### Instructions:
1. **Prepare the Lentils**: Rinse the lentils under cold water and set aside.
2. **Sauté the Aromatics**: In a pot, heat a small amount of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and minced garlic, and sauté until the onions are translucent.
3. **Add Vegetables**: Stir in the diced tomatoes and carrots, cooking for a few minutes until they soften.
4. **Cook the Lentils**: Add the lentils to the pot along with the spices and enough water or broth to cover the lentils. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
5. **Add Greens**: In the last few minutes of cooking, stir in the chopped spinach or kale until wilted.
6. **Serve**: Serve the lentil stew hot with injera or flatbread on the side.

### Why is it Healthy?
1. **High in Protein**: Lentils are an excellent source of plant-based protein, which is essential for muscle repair and overall health.
2. **Rich in Fiber**: Lentils and vegetables provide dietary fiber, which aids in digestion and helps maintain a healthy gut.
3. **Vitamins and Minerals**: The addition of vegetables like carrots and spinach adds essential vitamins (like A and C) and minerals (like iron and calcium) to your meal.
4. **Low in Cost**: Lentils and local vegetables are generally inexpensive and widely available, making this meal budget-friendly.
5. **Heart-Healthy**: The combination of lentils and vegetables can help lower cholesterol levels and reduce the risk of heart disease.

This meal is not only nutritious but also versatile; you can adjust the vegetables and spices based on what you have available or prefer. Enjoy your healthy dinner!
